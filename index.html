Pangong Lake

High altitude river

← →

A stunning high-altitude lake located at an elevation of 4,350 meters, Pangong Lake is known for its crystal-clear blue waters that change colors throughout the day. It spans India and China, with 60% of the lake in China.

Cold desert climate. Summers are mild, with daytime temperatures around 5°C to 20°C. Winters are harsh, with temperatures dropping as low as -25°C.

Best Time to Visit: May to September.

Indian Astronomical Observatory of Hanle, Ladakh

Hanle Observatory

Located just 10 kilometers from Leh city, the Leh Observatory is an ideal spot for stargazing. Nestled in the high-altitude desert of Ladakh, the observatory offers some of the clearest skies for celestial observations. The observatory is well-equipped with telescopes, and you can catch breathtaking views of stars, planets, and even the Milky Way!

Permit Information

To visit the Leh Observatory, you will need a special permit, which can be obtained at the local tourist office in Leh. The permit typically costs around ₹200 per person. Make sure to carry a valid ID (Aadhar or Passport) when applying for the permit.

Climate and Best Time to Visit

The climate in Leh is cold and dry, with temperatures ranging from 5°C to -15°C during stargazing months. The best time for stargazing is from September to March, when the skies are clearest and there is minimal light pollution. Remember to bundle up as temperatures can drop drastically at night!

Distance from Leh

The Leh Observatory is located just 10 km from Leh City. It’s a quick 20-minute drive, making it easily accessible for tourists staying in the city. Public transport and taxis are available for visitors.

Motor rides

Nubra Valley Circuit

Distance: 140 km

Difficulty: Moderate

Best Time: June to September

This circuit takes you through the stunning Nubra Valley, passing over the famous Khardung La Pass. Experience the unique landscapes and culture of the region.
Average Altitude: 3,048 meters (10,000 feet) above sea level
Temperature Range:
Summer (June to September): 10°C to 25°C (50°F to 77°F)
Winter (October to May): -5°C to -20°C (23°F to -4°F)
Pressure: Due to the high altitude, the air pressure is lower, but less extreme than higher passes like Khardung La. Acclimatization is still recommended.
Terrain: A mix of paved roads and rugged trails, with steep ascents and descents, especially over Khardung La.
Route Highlights:
Khardung La Pass: One of the highest motorable roads in the world at 5,359 meters (17,582 feet). The pass offers breathtaking views of the Himalayas and is a major attraction on the way to Nubra Valley.
Nubra Valley: Known for its unique landscape of sand dunes, lush green oasis, and the Shyok and Nubra rivers. The valley is often referred to as the "Valley of Flowers."
Diskit Monastery: The largest and oldest monastery in Nubra, home to a 32-meter tall Maitreya Buddha statue offering panoramic views of the valley.
Hunder Sand Dunes: Famous for its white sand dunes, where you can experience camel rides on double-humped Bactrian camels, unique to the region.
Cultural Experience: Explore the villages of Nubra Valley, including the towns of Hunder and Sumur, where you can witness the traditional Ladakhi way of life.

Pangong Lake Circuit

Distance: 160 km

Difficulty: Challenging

Best Time: May to October

Ride to the mesmerizing Pangong Lake, famous for its clear blue waters. The route takes you through rugged terrains and Chang La Pass.
Average Altitude: 4,350 meters (14,270 feet) above sea level
Temperature Range:
Summer (May to August): 5°C to 25°C (41°F to 77°F)
Winter (October to April): -10°C to -25°C (14°F to -13°F)
Pressure: Atmospheric pressure decreases significantly due to high altitude. Oxygen levels are lower, making acclimatization important.
Terrain: Rugged and off-road, crossing through Chang La Pass (5,360 meters), one of the highest motorable roads in the world.
Route Highlights:
Pangong Lake: Known for its mesmerizing blue waters, the lake's color changes throughout the day due to the sunlight.
Chang La Pass: A thrilling ride through snow-capped mountains and steep ascents.
Scenic Beauty: The route offers panoramic views of the Himalayas, barren landscapes, and occasional wildlife sightings, including marmots and wild yaks.

Tso Moriri Circuit

Distance: 220 km

Difficulty: Advanced

Best Time: June to September

This circuit takes you to the high-altitude Tso Moriri Lake, known for its peaceful surroundings and pristine beauty. It's a challenging route with rewarding views.
Average Altitude: 4,522 meters (14,836 feet) above sea level
Temperature Range:
Summer (June to September): 0°C to 20°C (32°F to 68°F)
Winter (October to May): -10°C to -30°C (14°F to -22°F)
Pressure: The high altitude results in lower atmospheric pressure and oxygen levels, making acclimatization essential.
Terrain: The route is mostly off-road with rough patches, steep climbs, and unpredictable weather conditions.
Route Highlights:
Tso Moriri Lake: A serene, high-altitude lake known for its crystal-clear waters and surrounding snow-capped mountains. The lake is a vital habitat for migratory birds and is part of the Tso Moriri Wetland Conservation Reserve.
Korzok Village: The highest settlement near the lake, inhabited by nomads. It offers a glimpse of the unique culture of the Changpa people.
Wildlife: The region is home to rare species such as Tibetan wolves, snow leopards, and the famous Kiang (Tibetan wild ass).
Scenic Views: Majestic landscapes with rugged mountains, open plains, and stunning vistas of the remote Ladakh region.
